Enterprise Network Infrastructure — EOU

Internship maintaining live campus network infrastructure at Eastern Oregon University, with hands-on responsibility for switching and firewall hardware.

Juniper Switches Firewalls Enterprise LAN

Role

Worked as an intern within EOU's network infrastructure team, supporting the switching and firewall hardware that keeps the campus network running for staff, faculty, and students.

What the work involved

  • Configuration and troubleshooting of Juniper switches in a live enterprise environment — not a lab simulation, but production hardware serving real users.
  • Maintenance of network firewall rules and hardware as part of the campus's day-to-day operations.
  • Exposure to how enterprise-scale LAN segments, uplinks, and switching decisions actually play out in practice, versus in coursework.

Why it matters

Most cybersecurity coursework treats networking as diagrams and theory. This internship put that theory in front of hardware that couldn't go down without affecting real people — which changes how carefully you think about every change before you make it.
